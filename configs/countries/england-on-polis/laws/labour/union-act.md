# Foxbridge Trade Union Act

*An Act regulating trade unions, recognition, collective bargaining, and industrial action in England-on-Polis. Adapted from the UK Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992 and Trade Union Act 2016 (OGL v3.0). Substrate-fictionalised per `polis/configs/sources.yaml`. Cross-references Foxbridge Employment Act Part 6.*

---

## Part 1 — Trade unions

### §1 Definition

A trade union is an organisation of workers whose principal purposes include the regulation of relations between workers and employers.

### §2 Listing and certification

(1) A trade union may apply to the **Foxbridge Certification Officer** to be listed.

(2) An independent listed trade union (one not under employer control or financial dependence on an employer) is entitled to a **certificate of independence**.

(3) Independence carries statutory rights to time off for trade-union duties, recognition procedures, and consultation rights.

### §3 Internal democracy

(1) Trade unions must:
  (a) hold elections for the governing body, president, and general secretary every 5 years;
  (b) maintain a register of members;
  (c) maintain audited accounts and submit them annually;
  (d) provide members with reasonable access to records.

(2) Members have rights to:
  (a) not be unjustifiably disciplined;
  (b) sue the union for breach of rule;
  (c) be informed of major union decisions.

## Part 2 — Recognition

### §4 Voluntary recognition

An employer may voluntarily recognise an independent trade union for purposes of collective bargaining.

### §5 Statutory recognition

(1) An independent trade union may apply for statutory recognition where:
  (a) the bargaining unit has 21 or more workers;
  (b) the union has the support of a majority of workers in the unit (assessed by membership audit or, if necessary, by ballot);
  (c) no competing recognition agreement is in force.

(2) Recognition obliges the employer to bargain on:
  (a) pay;
  (b) hours;
  (c) holidays;
  (d) other matters by agreement.

## Part 3 — Industrial action

### §6 Lawful industrial action

(1) Industrial action is lawful only where:
  (a) it is in furtherance of a **trade dispute** (a dispute between workers and their employer wholly or mainly relating to terms and conditions, dismissal, work allocation, etc);
  (b) the union has held a **postal ballot** with majority support of those voting and turnout of at least 50%;
  (c) for important public services (health, education, transport, fire, border), turnout AND a 40% YES-of-eligible threshold must both be met;
  (d) **7 days' notice** in writing has been given to the employer specifying the action.

(2) The ballot paper must clearly specify the kind of industrial action proposed, the period within which action will take place, and provide a yes/no choice.

### §7 Unlawful action

The following are not protected by trade-dispute immunity:
  (a) **secondary action** (action against an employer not party to the dispute);
  (b) **politically-motivated action** (action not principally about terms and conditions);
  (c) **wildcat strikes** (without ballot or notice);
  (d) action involving violence, intimidation, or interference with essential public safety;
  (e) action by trade unions in critical sectors that fails the §6(1)(c) thresholds.

### §8 Picketing

(1) Lawful picketing is peaceful, at or near one's own workplace, for the purpose of communicating information or persuading workers to work or not.

(2) The number of pickets at any entrance is recommended at no more than 6.

(3) Picket-line conduct breaching public order is not protected (cross-reference Foxbridge Criminal Code §13).

### §9 Liability for unlawful action

(1) An employer may seek an injunction against unlawful industrial action.

(2) Damages claims against trade unions are capped by the Foxbridge Trade Union (Compensation Cap) Order, scaled to membership size.

## Part 4 — Discrimination on union grounds

### §10 Right not to be detrimentally treated

A worker has the right not to be subjected to detriment, dismissed, or denied employment on the grounds of trade-union membership, non-membership, or lawful trade-union activity (cross-reference Foxbridge Employment Act §16).

## Part 5 — Football-specific provisions

### §11 Footballer collective representation

(1) The **Hartshire Footballers' Association** (HFA) is the recognised representative body for professional footballers in England-on-Polis.

(2) The HFA holds statutory recognition for collective bargaining on standard-form contract terms with the Federation of Hartshire and member-club bodies, but does not negotiate individual player contracts (those are individual employment relationships per Foxbridge Employment Act §19).

(3) Industrial action by professional footballers is theoretically permissible under §6 but is rare; collective dispute resolution is typically by Federation tribunal.

## Part 6 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §12 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) This Act may be cited as the **Foxbridge Trade Union Act**.

(2) This Act came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world.

(3) This Act extends to England-on-Polis only.
