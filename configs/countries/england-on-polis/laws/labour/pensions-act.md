# Foxbridge Pensions Act

*An Act consolidating workplace pension regulation in England-on-Polis: auto-enrolment, scheme governance, contribution rules, retirement ages. Adapted from the UK Pensions Acts 2004 and 2008, and Pension Schemes Act 1993 (OGL v3.0). Substrate-fictionalised per `polis/configs/sources.yaml`.*

---

## Part 1 — Workplace pensions

### §1 Auto-enrolment duty

(1) Every employer in England-on-Polis must:
  (a) automatically enrol eligible workers into a qualifying pension scheme;
  (b) contribute at least the statutory minimum;
  (c) re-enrol opted-out workers every 3 years.

(2) **Eligible worker**: aged 22 to state pension age, earning above ₽15,000 per year, working in England-on-Polis.

### §2 Minimum contributions

(1) Total minimum contribution (employer + employee + tax relief): **8% of qualifying earnings**.

(2) Of which:
  (a) employer: minimum **3%**;
  (b) employee: minimum **5%** (inclusive of basic-rate tax relief).

(3) Higher contributions by agreement permitted; employer may match contributions up to a stated cap.

### §3 Opt-out

A worker may opt out within one month of enrolment (full refund) or thereafter (contributions remain in scheme until normal retirement age).

## Part 2 — Scheme governance

### §4 Trustees

(1) Every occupational pension scheme must have **trustees** subject to the Foxbridge Property Act §11–§12 (trustee duties).

(2) At least **one-third member-nominated trustees** must be on the trustee body of any scheme with 100 or more members.

### §5 The Foxbridge Pensions Regulator

The **Foxbridge Pensions Regulator** has powers to:
  (a) issue codes of practice;
  (b) require disclosure of scheme information;
  (c) impose fines for non-compliance with auto-enrolment;
  (d) declare a scheme entry to the Pension Protection Fund where employer is insolvent;
  (e) issue contribution-notices to associated parties to make good underfunding caused by avoidance.

### §6 The Pension Protection Fund

(1) The Pension Protection Fund (PPF) provides compensation to members of eligible defined-benefit schemes whose employers become insolvent and whose schemes are underfunded.

(2) Funded by levy on eligible schemes; member compensation capped at 90% of accrued benefits up to a statutory cap.

## Part 3 — Member rights

### §7 Information

Members have the right to:
  (a) annual benefit statement;
  (b) member-facing scheme rulebook;
  (c) annual report and accounts;
  (d) explanation of charges (for defined-contribution schemes);
  (e) reasonable transfer-out information.

### §8 Transfers

A member may transfer accrued benefits between qualifying schemes. Defined-benefit transfers above ₽30,000 require regulated financial advice.

### §9 Retirement options (defined contribution)

At normal retirement age (or earlier per scheme rules):
  (a) **lump-sum** (up to 25% tax-free);
  (b) **drawdown** (flexible income from pension fund);
  (c) **annuity** (guaranteed income for life);
  (d) **combination**.

## Part 4 — State pension

### §10 State pension age

(1) The state pension age is **67**, increasing to **68** by a phased timetable to be set by the Treasury Minister with parliamentary notice.

(2) Eligibility requires at least **10 qualifying years** of national-insurance-equivalent contributions; **35 years** for the full new state pension.

(3) State pension is paid by Inland Revenue Foxbridge from general taxation per the Foxbridge Social Security Order.

## Part 5 — Football-specific provisions

### §11 Footballer pensions

(1) Footballers employed by licensed clubs are subject to the auto-enrolment duty in §1.

(2) Many clubs operate top-up schemes above the statutory minimum; such schemes are subject to this Act.

(3) **Career-ending injury**: a player whose career is terminated by injury covered by an industry-standard insurance scheme may continue contributions on the basis of that insurance payout per Federation of Hartshire rules supplementing this Act.

## Part 6 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §12 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) This Act may be cited as the **Foxbridge Pensions Act**.

(2) This Act came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world.

(3) This Act extends to England-on-Polis only.
