# Foxbridge Criminal Procedure Rules

*Procedural framework for criminal cases in England-on-Polis: investigation, charge, pre-trial, trial, appeal, sentence-execution. Adapted from the UK's Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984, Criminal Procedure Rules, Criminal Justice Act 2003, and Sentencing Act 2020 (OGL v3.0). Substrate-fictionalised per `polis/configs/sources.yaml`.*

---

## Part 1 — Investigation

### §1 Powers of arrest

(1) A police officer may arrest without warrant where:
  (a) they have reasonable grounds to suspect that an arrestable offence has been, is being, or is about to be committed by the person; and
  (b) arrest is **necessary** for one of the lawful reasons (preserve evidence, protect a person, prevent loss of identity).

(2) An arrest must be promptly explained to the suspect, who must be cautioned.

### §2 The caution

The Foxbridge caution is: *"You do not have to say anything. But it may harm your defence if you do not mention when questioned something you later rely on in court. Anything you do say may be given in evidence."*

### §3 Detention and questioning

(1) A person may be detained for questioning for up to **24 hours** without charge; extendable to **96 hours** with judicial authorisation.

(2) Police interviews must be recorded (audio/video). The suspect has the right to legal advice and to have a lawyer present.

(3) Vulnerable suspects (children, persons with mental health conditions) must have an **appropriate adult** present.

### §4 Searches and seizures

(1) Search of premises requires a warrant from a magistrate or, in narrow circumstances, an officer of inspector rank or above.

(2) Stop and search of persons and vehicles is permitted on reasonable grounds, with safeguards: identification, reason, recording.

(3) Material seized must be returned at the conclusion of the case unless retained by court order.

## Part 2 — Charge

### §5 Decision to charge

(1) The decision to charge is made by the **Foxbridge Crown Prosecution Service** applying:
  (a) the **evidential test**: realistic prospect of conviction;
  (b) the **public-interest test**: prosecution is in the public interest.

(2) The Crown Prosecution Service is independent of the Justice Minister in individual case decisions (per Foxbridge Justice Minister cabinet-position constraints).

### §6 Charge and bail

(1) On charge, the suspect is either released on bail (with or without conditions) or held in custody pending court appearance.

(2) Bail conditions may include reporting requirements, residence restrictions, electronic monitoring, surrender of passport, no-contact orders.

## Part 3 — Pre-trial

### §7 Mode of trial

(1) Offences are categorised as:
  (a) **summary** (tried in a Magistrates' Court without jury);
  (b) **either-way** (tried summarily or on indictment, depending on seriousness and election);
  (c) **indictable-only** (tried in the Crown Court of Foxbridge with jury).

### §8 Disclosure

(1) The prosecution must disclose all material that might reasonably be considered capable of undermining the prosecution case or assisting the defence.

(2) The defence must serve a defence statement specifying the issues in dispute, alibi witnesses, and any objections to prosecution evidence.

### §9 Plea and case management

(1) The accused enters a plea (guilty / not guilty) at the first hearing.

(2) On a guilty plea, sentencing follows; on a not-guilty plea, the case proceeds to trial preparation.

(3) Plea reductions: a guilty plea at the first reasonable opportunity attracts up to a one-third reduction in sentence; reductions taper as proceedings progress.

## Part 4 — Trial

### §10 Burden and standard of proof

The prosecution bears the burden of proof; the standard is **beyond reasonable doubt**.

### §11 Conduct of trial

(1) Trial proceeds with prosecution case (evidence-in-chief, cross-examination, re-examination), defence case, closing speeches, judicial directions to the jury (where applicable), verdict.

(2) The jury (in Crown Court) must be 12 persons; verdict ideally unanimous, with majority verdicts (10–2 or 11–1) permissible after a minimum deliberation period.

(3) The accused has the right not to give evidence; adverse inferences may be drawn in narrow circumstances (per the caution at §2).

### §12 Hearsay and bad character

(1) Hearsay is inadmissible save under specific statutory exceptions or in the interests of justice.

(2) Bad character (previous convictions, propensity) is inadmissible save under the **gateways** in the Foxbridge Criminal Justice Act.

## Part 5 — Sentencing

### §13 Sentencing hearing

(1) On conviction, the court holds a sentencing hearing considering:
  (a) the circumstances of the offence;
  (b) the offender's antecedents and personal circumstances;
  (c) any victim impact statement;
  (d) any pre-sentence report from probation;
  (e) sentencing guidelines.

### §14 Range of sentences

(1) **Custodial**: prison sentence; suspended sentence; intermittent custody.

(2) **Community**: unpaid work; rehabilitation activity; mental-health, drug, or alcohol treatment; curfew; exclusion zone.

(3) **Financial**: fine, compensation order, confiscation order.

(4) **Ancillary**: driving disqualification, football banning order (per Foxbridge Football Act §13), sex offender notification, restraining order.

### §15 Custody threshold

A custodial sentence may be imposed only if the offence (or combination) is so serious that neither a fine nor community sentence can be justified.

### §16 Maximum sentences

Set by the offence-creating Acts; aggregated where multiple offences but subject to the totality principle (the overall sentence must reflect the overall offending without unfairness).

## Part 6 — Appeal

### §17 Appeals from Magistrates' Court

(1) **Crown Court appeal**: against conviction (rehearing) and/or sentence.

(2) **Case stated to High Court**: on a point of law.

(3) **Judicial review**: where procedure was so seriously flawed that it amounted to an abuse of process.

### §18 Appeals from Crown Court

(1) **Court of Appeal of Foxbridge** hears appeals against conviction (on grounds the verdict is unsafe) and sentence (on grounds it is manifestly excessive or wrong in principle). Permission to appeal is required.

(2) Further appeal to the **Supreme Court of Foxbridge** is by leave on points of law of general public importance.

## Part 7 — Special procedures

### §19 Vulnerable witnesses

(1) Children, persons with mental-health conditions, sexual-offence complainants, and intimidated witnesses qualify for **special measures** (live link, video-recorded evidence-in-chief, screens, removal of wigs and gowns).

### §20 Anonymity

(1) Sexual-offence complainants are entitled to lifelong anonymity.

(2) Children involved in proceedings (as victims, witnesses, or defendants) have anonymity until 18, save where lifted by court order.

### §21 Sport-integrity special procedures

Match-fixing, insider-betting, and bribery cases involving regulated sport may be tried before the **Crown Court of Foxbridge with sport-integrity directions**, including expert assessors. Federation of Hartshire disciplinary proceedings run in parallel and do not bar criminal prosecution.

## Part 8 — Restorative justice and out-of-court

### §22 Cautions and conditional cautions

For minor offences, the police may issue a simple caution (admitted offence, recorded) or a conditional caution (with rehabilitative or reparative conditions).

### §23 Restorative justice

A victim-led process available pre-charge, post-charge, and post-conviction; not an alternative to sentence but a complement to it.

## Part 9 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §24 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) These Rules may be cited as the **Foxbridge Criminal Procedure Rules**.

(2) These Rules came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world.

(3) These Rules extend to England-on-Polis only.
