# Foxbridge Criminal Code

*A consolidating statute of the principal criminal offences and general principles in England-on-Polis. Adapted from the UK's Theft Act 1968, Offences Against the Person Act 1861, Sexual Offences Act 2003, Misuse of Drugs Act 1971, Fraud Act 2006, and supporting case-law (OGL v3.0 for statutes; common-law principles freely adaptable). ## Part 1 — General principles

### §1 Criminal liability

(1) Criminal liability requires proof beyond reasonable doubt of:
  (a) an **actus reus** (a guilty act or omission);
  (b) the requisite **mens rea** (mental state) for the offence;
  (c) the absence of any complete defence.

(2) The burden of proof rests on the prosecution save where statute or established common-law doctrine reverses it.

(3) Strict-liability offences require proof of the actus reus only, but should be construed narrowly and presumed not to apply unless Parliament has clearly so provided.

### §2 Mental states

(1) **Intention**: the defendant's purpose was to bring about the consequence, or it was a virtual certainty and they appreciated this.

(2) **Recklessness**: the defendant foresaw a risk of the consequence and unreasonably took it.

(3) **Negligence**: the defendant fell below the standard of the reasonable person.

(4) **Knowledge**: the defendant was aware of the relevant fact, or wilfully blind to it.

### §3 Capacity and infancy

(1) A child under **10 years** is conclusively presumed incapable of crime (the age of criminal responsibility).

(2) A person under 18 may be tried but is sentenced under the Foxbridge Youth Justice provisions (lower tariffs, rehabilitation focus).

(3) The defence of **insanity** is available where, at the time of the act, the defendant was suffering from a defect of reason from disease of the mind such that they did not know the nature and quality of their act or did not know it was wrong.

(4) **Diminished responsibility** is a partial defence to murder, reducing it to manslaughter, where the defendant was suffering from an abnormality of mental functioning substantially impairing responsibility.

## Part 2 — Offences against the person

### §4 Murder and manslaughter

(1) **Murder** is the unlawful killing of a human being with intent to kill or to cause grievous bodily harm. Sentence: **life imprisonment**, with a minimum tariff set by the trial judge.

(2) **Voluntary manslaughter** is murder reduced by partial defence (loss of control, diminished responsibility, suicide pact).

(3) **Involuntary manslaughter** is unlawful killing without the murder mens rea, by:
  (a) unlawful and dangerous act;
  (b) gross negligence;
  (c) reckless conduct.

### §5 Assault and battery

(1) **Common assault**: intentionally or recklessly causing another to apprehend immediate unlawful violence.

(2) **Battery**: intentionally or recklessly applying unlawful force to another. Maximum: 6 months' imprisonment.

(3) **Actual bodily harm (ABH)**: assault occasioning hurt or injury that interferes with health or comfort. Maximum: 5 years.

(4) **Grievous bodily harm (GBH)**: causing serious harm — wounding (broken skin) or really serious injury. Maximum (without intent): 5 years; **with intent**: life imprisonment.

### §6 Sexual offences

(1) **Rape**: intentional penetration of the vagina, anus, or mouth of another with a penis without consent and without reasonable belief in consent. Sentence: maximum life imprisonment.

(2) **Sexual assault**: intentional sexual touching without consent. Maximum: 10 years.

(3) **Sexual offences against children**: a separate, more severe regime applies to offences against persons under 16.

(4) **CSAM** (child sexual abuse material): possession, distribution, and creation are criminal offences with maximum sentences of 10 to 14 years. *Engine note: substrate engine refuses any such content at the model layer per Constitution Article XI; criminal law is the country-level redundant guard.*

### §7 Domestic abuse and coercive control

(1) Repeated patterns of controlling, coercive, threatening, degrading, or violent behaviour against an intimate partner or family member is the offence of **coercive control**.

(2) Maximum: 5 years.

## Part 3 — Property offences

### §8 Theft

(1) A person is guilty of theft if they dishonestly appropriate property belonging to another with the intention of permanently depriving the other of it.

(2) Maximum: 7 years.

### §9 Robbery

Theft accompanied by force or threat of force. Maximum: life imprisonment.

### §10 Burglary

Entering a building as a trespasser with intent to commit theft, GBH, or criminal damage. Maximum: 14 years (dwelling), 10 years (other).

### §11 Fraud

(1) **Fraud by false representation**: dishonestly making a false representation, intending to make a gain or cause loss.

(2) **Fraud by failing to disclose** information one is under a legal duty to disclose.

(3) **Fraud by abuse of position**: dishonestly abusing a position in which one is expected to safeguard another's financial interests.

(4) Maximum: 10 years.

### §12 Money laundering

Concealing, converting, transferring, or removing criminal property; or entering into arrangements concerning criminal property; or acquiring, using, or possessing criminal property. Maximum: 14 years.

## Part 4 — Public-order and integrity offences

### §13 Public-order offences

(1) **Riot**: 12 or more persons present together using or threatening unlawful violence, with common purpose.

(2) **Violent disorder**: 3 or more persons using or threatening unlawful violence.

(3) **Affray**: using or threatening unlawful violence such that a person of reasonable firmness would fear for their personal safety.

(4) **Threatening, abusive, or insulting conduct** likely to cause harassment, alarm, or distress.

### §14 Sport-specific public-order offences

Cross-reference: Foxbridge Football Act Part 5 (banning orders, racial chanting, pitch invasion, drink and drugs in stadia).

### §15 Sporting integrity offences

(1) **Match-fixing**: any conduct intended to influence the outcome of a regulated sporting contest for betting or other consideration. Maximum: 7 years.

(2) **Insider betting**: a player, manager, agent, or club employee placing or arranging a bet on a contest in which their club participates. Maximum: 5 years.

(3) **Tipping**: disclosure of confidential club information for betting advantage. Maximum: 5 years.

### §16 Bribery

(1) Offering, giving, or receiving a financial or other advantage with the intention of inducing or rewarding the improper performance of a function or activity.

(2) Maximum: 10 years.

(3) Applies to public officials, private-sector counterparts, and football-club officials.

## Part 5 — Drugs and intoxicants

### §17 Controlled drugs

The Foxbridge Misuse of Drugs Order classifies controlled drugs into Class A (most serious), Class B, and Class C, with offences of possession, supply, and production carrying tariff sentences scaled to class.

## Part 6 — Computer and data offences

### §18 Computer offences

Unauthorised access to a computer system, unauthorised modification of computer material, and the making, supplying, or obtaining of articles for use in computer offences are criminal offences. Maximum: 2 to 10 years depending on aggravation.

### §19 Data protection offences

Unlawful obtaining, disclosure, or sale of personal data. Cross-reference: Foxbridge Data Protection Act.

## Part 7 — Driving and transport

### §20 Driving offences

Causing death by dangerous driving (max: 14 years), causing serious injury by dangerous driving, dangerous driving simpliciter, careless driving, drink driving, drug driving — all per the Foxbridge Road Traffic Order.

## Part 8 — Defences

### §21 General defences

(1) **Self-defence**: proportionate force against a threat to self or another.

(2) **Defence of property**: proportionate force to protect property.

(3) **Duress**: threats of death or serious harm compelling the act, where a reasonable person of similar characteristics would have so acted.

(4) **Necessity**: limited common-law defence; rare; not available to murder.

(5) **Consent**: a defence to non-fatal violence offences where the conduct is lawful (sport within rules, surgery, tattooing) but not generally to GBH save in recognised contexts.

(6) **Mistake**: an honest mistake of fact may negate mens rea.

(7) **Automatism**: complete loss of voluntary control negates actus reus.

## Part 9 — Sentencing principles

### §22 Sentencing aims

The aims of sentencing are:
  (a) punishment proportionate to the offence;
  (b) reduction of crime (including by deterrence);
  (c) reform and rehabilitation;
  (d) protection of the public;
  (e) reparation by the offender to victims and the community.

### §23 Aggravating and mitigating factors

Aggravating factors include premeditation, vulnerability of victim, abuse of trust, hostility on prohibited grounds, breach of bail. Mitigating factors include genuine remorse, prompt admission, age, lack of previous convictions.

### §24 Sentencing tariff

Statutory minima and maxima are as set out in the offence-creating provisions; the Sentencing Council of Foxbridge issues guidelines.

## Part 10 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §25 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) This Act may be cited as the **Foxbridge Criminal Code**.

(2) This Act came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world.

(3) This Act extends to England-on-Polis only.
