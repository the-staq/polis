# Foxbridge Defamation Act

*An Act to amend the law of defamation in England-on-Polis. Adapted from the Defamation Act 2013 of the United Kingdom (Crown Copyright, Open Government Licence v3.0). Substrate-fictionalised per `polis/configs/sources.yaml`. All real-world references substituted; the Act takes the substrate-fictional name "Foxbridge Defamation Act."*

---

## Part 1 — Requirement of serious harm

### §1 Serious harm

(1) A statement is not defamatory unless its publication has caused or is likely to cause **serious harm** to the reputation of the claimant.

(2) For the purposes of this section, harm to the reputation of a body that trades for profit (including football clubs, corporations, and other commercial institutions registered in England-on-Polis) is not "serious harm" unless it has caused or is likely to cause the body **serious financial loss**.

*Engine note: ConflictWorkflow uses §1 as the entry threshold for any defamation claim raised by a character or institution. Below this threshold, the engine refuses to escalate the dispute to court, regardless of subjective offence taken.*

## Part 2 — Defences

### §2 Truth

(1) It is a defence to an action for defamation for the defendant to show that the imputation conveyed by the statement complained of is **substantially true**.

(2) Subject to subsection (3), if one or more of the imputations is not shown to be substantially true, the defence under this section does not fail if, having regard to the imputations which are shown to be substantially true, the imputations which are not shown to be substantially true do not seriously harm the claimant's reputation.

### §3 Honest opinion

(1) It is a defence to an action for defamation for the defendant to show that the conditions in subsections (2) to (4) are met.

(2) The first condition is that the statement complained of was a **statement of opinion**.

(3) The second condition is that the statement complained of indicated, whether in general or specific terms, the basis of the opinion.

(4) The third condition is that an honest person could have held the opinion on the basis of (a) any fact which existed at the time the statement complained of was published, or (b) anything asserted to be a fact in a privileged publication published before the statement complained of.

*Engine note: §3 is the defence Echo Foxbridge News relies on when its analysts publish performance critique. The "honest person could have held" test is judged by the LLM-judge in ConflictWorkflow against the cited basis facts.*

### §4 Publication on matter of public interest

(1) It is a defence to an action for defamation for the defendant to show that —
  (a) the statement complained of was, or formed part of, a statement on a matter of **public interest**, and
  (b) the defendant **reasonably believed** that publishing the statement complained of was in the public interest.

(2) Subject to subsections (3) and (4), in determining whether the defendant has shown the matters mentioned in subsection (1), the court must have regard to all the circumstances of the case.

(3) If the statement complained of was, or formed part of, an accurate and impartial account of a dispute to which the claimant was a party, the court must in determining whether it was reasonable for the defendant to believe that publishing the statement was in the public interest disregard any omission of the defendant to take steps to verify the truth of the imputation conveyed by it.

### §5 Operators of websites

(1) This section applies where an action for defamation is brought against the operator of a website in respect of a statement posted on the website.

(2) It is a defence for the operator to show that it was not the operator who posted the statement on the website.

(3) The defence is defeated if the claimant shows that —
  (a) it was not possible for the claimant to identify the person who posted the statement,
  (b) the claimant gave the operator a notice of complaint in relation to the statement, and
  (c) the operator failed to respond to the notice of complaint in accordance with any provision contained in regulations.

*Substrate note: §5 in England-on-Polis applies to in-world content platforms (e.g., GoalCast, FoxFeed). The substrate engine itself is not a "website operator" in the meaning of this section; the engine is constitutional infrastructure under Article XI of the Constitution.*

## Part 3 — Privilege

### §6 Peer-reviewed statement in scientific or academic journal

(1) The publication of a statement in a scientific or academic journal (whether published in electronic form or otherwise) is privileged if the following conditions are met.

(2) The first condition is that the statement relates to a scientific or academic matter.

(3) The second condition is that before the statement was published in the journal an independent review of the statement's scientific or academic merit was carried out by — (a) the editor of the journal, and (b) one or more persons with expertise in the scientific or academic matter concerned.

### §7 Reports etc protected by privilege

(1) Fair and accurate reports of court proceedings, parliamentary proceedings, and proceedings of public meetings of public bodies in England-on-Polis are protected by **absolute privilege** when contemporaneous and **qualified privilege** otherwise.

(2) Privilege under this section is defeated by malice in fact.

## Part 4 — Procedure

### §8 Single publication rule

(1) This section applies if a person —
  (a) publishes a statement to the public ("the first publication"), and
  (b) subsequently publishes (whether or not to the public) that statement or a statement which is substantially the same.

(2) In subsection (1) "publication to the public" includes publication to a section of the public.

(3) The cause of action against the person in respect of the subsequent publication is to be treated as having accrued on the date of the first publication.

(4) Subsection (3) does not apply in relation to the subsequent publication if the manner of that publication is materially different from the manner of the first publication.

### §9 Action against person not domiciled in England-on-Polis

(1) This section applies to an action for defamation against a person who is not domiciled in England-on-Polis or any country recognised under §8 of the Treaties Act.

(2) A court does not have jurisdiction to hear and determine an action to which this section applies unless the court is satisfied that, of all the places in which the statement complained of has been published, England-on-Polis is **clearly the most appropriate place** in which to bring an action in respect of the statement.

*V0 note: at V0 there is only one country, so §9 is dormant. Activated when other countries spawn at V1+.*

### §10 Action against person who was not the author, editor etc

(1) A court does not have jurisdiction to hear and determine an action for defamation brought against a person who was not the author, editor, or publisher of the statement complained of unless the court is satisfied that it is not reasonably practicable for an action to be brought against the author, editor, or publisher.

### §11 Trial without jury

(1) Defamation cases shall be tried without a jury unless the court orders otherwise. (Reflects the post-2013 default; juries remain available in exceptional cases by judicial discretion.)

### §12 Power of court to order summary of judgment to be published

The court may order the defendant to publish a summary of the court's judgment in the proceedings. The wording of any summary and the manner, time, and place of its publication shall be for the parties to agree, failing which the court shall determine.

### §13 Order to remove statement etc

(1) Where a court gives judgment for the claimant in an action for defamation, the court may order —
  (a) the operator of a website on which the defamatory statement is posted to remove the statement;
  (b) any person who was not the author, editor, or publisher of the defamatory statement to stop distributing, selling, or exhibiting material containing the statement.

### §14 Special damage

In an action for slander the claimant must prove special damage unless the publication imputes —
  (a) a criminal offence punishable with imprisonment;
  (b) a contagious disease likely to cause exclusion from society;
  (c) unfitness, dishonesty, or incompetence in any office, profession, calling, trade, or business held or carried on by the claimant at the time of the publication;
  (d) unchastity in any person.

## Part 5 — Relationship with the press

### §15 Editorial standards and the Independent Press Standards Organisation of Foxbridge (IPSO Foxbridge)

(1) Conformity with the IPSO Foxbridge Editors' Code of Practice is a relevant fact in determining the reasonable-belief test under §4(2). It is not determinative.

(2) IPSO Foxbridge maintains a complaints process. A complaint to IPSO Foxbridge does not bar a subsequent civil action in defamation.

*Engine note: ConflictWorkflow checks IPSO Foxbridge complaint history when adjudicating §4 reasonable-belief, but does not require it as precondition.*

## Part 6 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §16 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) This Act may be cited as the **Foxbridge Defamation Act**.

(2) This Act came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world (founding date of England-on-Polis).

(3) This Act extends to England-on-Polis only.
