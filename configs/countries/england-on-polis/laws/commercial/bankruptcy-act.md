# Foxbridge Bankruptcy Act

*An Act consolidating personal insolvency and corporate insolvency in England-on-Polis. Adapted from the UK Insolvency Act 1986 and Insolvency Rules 2016 (OGL v3.0). Substrate-fictionalised per `polis/configs/sources.yaml`.*

---

## Part 1 — Tests of insolvency

### §1 Cash-flow test

A debtor is insolvent if unable to pay debts as they fall due.

### §2 Balance-sheet test

A debtor is insolvent if liabilities exceed assets, taking into account contingent and prospective liabilities.

### §3 Statutory demand

Service of a statutory demand for an undisputed debt above ₽5,000 (individuals) or ₽10,000 (companies) which remains unpaid for 21 days creates a presumption of insolvency.

## Part 2 — Personal insolvency

### §4 Bankruptcy

(1) Bankruptcy may be commenced by debtor petition or creditor petition based on a statutory demand.

(2) Order of bankruptcy:
  (a) the debtor's assets vest in a **trustee in bankruptcy**;
  (b) reasonable living expenses retained;
  (c) discharge typically after **1 year** subject to extension on grounds of misconduct;
  (d) bankruptcy restrictions order may extend disclosure and credit-restriction obligations to 15 years.

### §5 Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

A negotiated settlement with creditors approved by 75% by value, supervised by a licensed insolvency practitioner. IVA bars enforcement actions while in force.

### §6 Debt Relief Order

For low-asset, low-income debtors with debts under a statutory threshold (₽30,000), administered by the Foxbridge Insolvency Service.

## Part 3 — Corporate insolvency

### §7 Administration

(1) Administration is a court-supervised process aimed at:
  (a) rescuing the company as a going concern; or
  (b) achieving a better result for creditors than immediate liquidation; or
  (c) realising property for distribution to secured/preferential creditors.

(2) An administrator is appointed by the court, by qualifying floating-charge holder, or by the company / directors.

### §8 Company Voluntary Arrangement (CVA)

A binding agreement between a company and its creditors approved by 75% by value of unsecured creditors. Used in retail and football-club restructurings.

### §9 Liquidation

(1) **Voluntary liquidation**: shareholder-initiated.
  (a) Members' voluntary (solvent): declaration of solvency; surplus to members.
  (b) Creditors' voluntary (insolvent): no declaration; creditors approve liquidator.

(2) **Compulsory liquidation**: court order on petition by creditor, member, regulator, or Crown.

### §10 Receivership

A secured creditor may appoint a receiver to enforce a fixed or floating charge. Subject to statutory duties of care.

## Part 4 — Order of distribution

### §11 Order of priority

In any insolvency, distributions are made in the following order:
  (a) costs and expenses of the insolvency;
  (b) **secured creditors** (to the value of their security);
  (c) **preferential creditors**:
     (i) employees' wages and holiday pay (capped per claim);
     (ii) certain pension contributions;
     (iii) Inland Revenue Foxbridge tax claims (Crown preference);
  (d) **floating-charge creditors** (subject to a prescribed-part fund for unsecured creditors);
  (e) **unsecured creditors** (pari passu);
  (f) **subordinated creditors** (per contract);
  (g) **shareholders** (residual).

## Part 5 — Antecedent transactions

### §12 Transactions at undervalue and preferences

(1) An office-holder may apply to set aside:
  (a) **transactions at undervalue** entered up to 2 years pre-insolvency (5 years if associated party);
  (b) **preferences** (favouring one creditor) entered up to 6 months pre-insolvency (2 years if associated party);
  (c) **transactions defrauding creditors** at any time.

### §13 Wrongful and fraudulent trading

(1) **Wrongful trading**: a director who knew or ought to have concluded that there was no reasonable prospect of avoiding insolvent liquidation, but failed to take every step to minimise loss to creditors, may be held personally liable.

(2) **Fraudulent trading**: carrying on business with intent to defraud creditors. Civil liability + criminal offence (cross-reference Foxbridge Criminal Code §11).

## Part 6 — Football-specific provisions

### §14 Football clubs in insolvency

(1) A licensed football club entering insolvency must promptly notify:
  (a) the Federation of Hartshire;
  (b) the league in which it competes;
  (c) the Sport Minister.

(2) A 10-point deduction is imposed by the Federation on a club entering administration during a season, deducted from the current season's points total.

(3) **Football creditors rule** (transitional from prior regimes): claims of players, managers, and other clubs rank ahead of unsecured trade creditors in any CVA or scheme. *This rule is contested as anticompetitive and is subject to Foxbridge Competition Commission review at V1+; V0 retains it for legal compatibility with prior fixture data.*

### §15 Heritage protection in insolvency

The grounds, kit, name, and badge of a football club entering insolvency may not be sold to a third party without consultation with the supporters' trust per Foxbridge Football Act §17.

## Part 7 — Cross-border insolvency

### §16 Recognition

V0: dormant. V1+ when other countries spawn: recognition of foreign-country insolvency proceedings will follow the Foxbridge Cross-Border Insolvency Order.

## Part 8 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §17 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) This Act may be cited as the **Foxbridge Bankruptcy Act**.

(2) This Act came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world.

(3) This Act extends to England-on-Polis only.
