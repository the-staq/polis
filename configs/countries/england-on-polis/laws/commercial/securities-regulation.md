# Foxbridge Securities Regulation

*Regulation of public offers, listed securities, market conduct, and disclosure in England-on-Polis. Adapted from the UK Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, Listing Rules, Disclosure and Transparency Rules (OGL v3.0). Substrate-fictionalised per `polis/configs/sources.yaml`.*

---

## Part 1 — Authority and scope

### §1 The Foxbridge Conduct Authority (FCA-Foxbridge)

(1) The Foxbridge Conduct Authority (`foxbridge-conduct-authority`) is the competent authority for:
  (a) admission of securities to listing on the Foxbridge Stock Exchange;
  (b) prospectus approval for public offers;
  (c) market-abuse and disclosure regulation;
  (d) financial-promotion oversight;
  (e) conduct of authorised firms providing investment services.

(2) The FCA-Foxbridge is independent of the Treasury Minister in operational decisions.

### §2 The Foxbridge Stock Exchange (FSX)

(1) The FSX is the principal regulated market for securities in England-on-Polis.

(2) Admission to the Main Market requires compliance with the Listing Rules; admission to the Growth Market requires compliance with the Growth Market Rules (lighter touch).

## Part 2 — Public offers and prospectus

### §3 Prospectus requirement

(1) An offer of transferable securities to the public requires an FCA-Foxbridge-approved prospectus, save where an exemption applies (qualified investors, fewer than 150 persons, minimum denomination ≥ ₽100,000, etc).

(2) The prospectus must include all information necessary for an informed assessment of:
  (a) the issuer's assets, liabilities, financial position, profits, and prospects;
  (b) the rights attaching to the securities.

(3) Liability for false or misleading statements in a prospectus rests with the issuer, the directors, and any expert who has consented to the inclusion of their statement.

### §4 Continuing disclosure

(1) Listed issuers must:
  (a) disclose **inside information** (price-sensitive non-public information) without delay;
  (b) maintain insider lists;
  (c) report directors' transactions;
  (d) publish annual and half-year financial reports;
  (e) hold an annual general meeting (per Foxbridge Corporations Act §8(2)).

(2) Selective disclosure to analysts or favoured investors is prohibited.

## Part 3 — Market abuse

### §5 Market abuse

(1) Market abuse comprises:
  (a) **insider dealing**: dealing on the basis of inside information;
  (b) **unlawful disclosure**: disclosing inside information outside the proper course of one's duties;
  (c) **market manipulation**: transactions or orders that give false or misleading signals as to supply, demand, or price; or that secure the price at an artificial level; or that employ fictitious devices.

(2) Civil penalties may be imposed by the FCA-Foxbridge. Criminal offences are charged by the Foxbridge Crown Prosecution Service per the Foxbridge Criminal Code.

### §6 Short selling

Net short positions of 0.2% or more of issued shares of a listed company must be reported to the FCA-Foxbridge; positions of 0.5% or more must be publicly disclosed.

## Part 4 — Authorised firms

### §7 Permission requirement

(1) No person may carry on a regulated activity in England-on-Polis without authorisation from the FCA-Foxbridge or an exemption.

(2) Regulated activities include accepting deposits, dealing in investments, advising on investments, managing investments, operating a multilateral trading facility, and providing custody services.

### §8 Firm conduct rules

Authorised firms must:
  (a) act with integrity;
  (b) act with due skill, care, and diligence;
  (c) take reasonable care to organise and control affairs responsibly;
  (d) maintain adequate financial resources;
  (e) observe proper standards of market conduct;
  (f) pay due regard to customers' interests and treat them fairly;
  (g) communicate with customers clearly, fairly, and not misleadingly;
  (h) take reasonable care to ensure suitability of advice;
  (i) arrange adequate protection for customer assets;
  (j) deal with regulators in an open and cooperative way.

### §9 Senior managers regime

(1) Specified senior management functions in authorised firms require pre-approval by the FCA-Foxbridge.

(2) Senior managers are personally accountable for prescribed responsibilities and must take reasonable steps to prevent regulatory breaches in their area.

## Part 5 — Takeovers

### §10 Foxbridge Takeover Code

(1) The Foxbridge Takeover Code applies to offers for shares of public companies and certain private companies with public-investor characteristics.

(2) Core principles include:
  (a) all holders of the same class to be afforded equivalent treatment;
  (b) the offeree's board to act in the interests of the company as a whole and not deny shareholders the opportunity to consider the merits;
  (c) sufficient time and information for shareholders to reach a properly informed decision.

(3) **Mandatory bid threshold**: a person (alone or with concert parties) acquiring 30% or more of voting rights must make a cash offer for the remaining shares at the highest price paid in the preceding 12 months.

## Part 6 — Football-specific (where listed)

### §11 Listed football clubs

(1) Where a football club is listed on the FSX, it must comply with this Regulation in addition to the Foxbridge Football Act fit-and-proper-persons regime.

(2) Where club ownership thresholds under Football Act §20 are crossed, the club must promptly disclose the material change as inside information per §4(1)(a).

(3) Heritage-protection covenants under Football Act §17 are pre-existing material commitments and must be disclosed in any prospectus.

## Part 7 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §12 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) This Regulation may be cited as **Foxbridge Securities Regulation**.

(2) This Regulation came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world.

(3) This Regulation extends to England-on-Polis only.
