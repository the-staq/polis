# Foxbridge Property Act

*An Act consolidating the law of real and personal property in England-on-Polis. Adapted from the Law of Property Act 1925, Land Registration Act 2002, and Torrens-equivalent registration regimes (UK, Open Government Licence v3.0). Substrate-fictionalised per `polis/configs/sources.yaml`.*

---

## Part 1 — Categories of property

### §1 Real and personal property

(1) **Real property** comprises land, buildings, and fixtures attached to the land.

(2) **Personal property** comprises everything else, including:
  (a) **chattels** (tangible movables);
  (b) **choses in action** (intangibles such as debts, shares, intellectual-property rights);
  (c) **digital assets** (POL balances, substrate-issued tokens, on-chain registered NFTs).

*Substrate note: at V0, every substrate-instance asset (POL balance, character, institution share, lot) is personal property of class (c). Real-world land equivalents in this country are abstract `lot` instances per `polis/schemas/lot.schema.json`.*

### §2 Estates and interests in land

(1) The two estates in land are **freehold** (estate in fee simple absolute in possession) and **leasehold** (term of years absolute).

(2) Lesser interests include easements, profits à prendre, restrictive covenants, mortgages, and rights of common.

(3) An interest in land is created or transferred only by deed registered at the **Foxbridge Land Registry** (or by short-term unregistered means for leases under 7 years).

## Part 2 — Acquisition

### §3 Sale of land

A sale of land in England-on-Polis requires:
  (a) a written contract per Foxbridge Contract Act §2(2)(a);
  (b) exchange of contracts and payment of the deposit;
  (c) execution of the deed of transfer;
  (d) registration of the transfer at the Foxbridge Land Registry;
  (e) payment of any property-transfer tax due to Inland Revenue Foxbridge.

### §4 Adverse possession

Continuous, exclusive, peaceful, open possession of land for **12 years** without the registered owner's permission may give rise to a claim for registration as registered owner, subject to any objection by the registered owner notified during the registration process.

### §5 Inheritance

(1) Real and personal property pass on death by:
  (a) **will**, if the deceased has executed a valid testamentary instrument;
  (b) **intestacy rules** (Foxbridge Inheritance Act), if no valid will.

(2) A footballer's contractual rights and image-rights companies are property within (1)(a) and (b) and pass accordingly, subject to anti-avoidance provisions in the Foxbridge Income Tax (Earnings) Act.

## Part 3 — Use and enjoyment

### §6 Quiet enjoyment

A registered owner has the right to quiet enjoyment of their property, subject to:
  (a) the rights of others (easements, covenants, statutory rights);
  (b) public-law restrictions (planning, building control, environmental);
  (c) the engine hard floors of Constitution Article XI.

### §7 Nuisance

A landowner is liable in nuisance to a neighbour materially affected by:
  (a) physical damage to property;
  (b) substantial interference with reasonable use and enjoyment;
  (c) statutory nuisance under the Foxbridge Public Health Act.

Reasonable measures of self-help and seeking court injunction are both available.

### §8 Negligence and occupier's liability

A landowner owes a duty of reasonable care to:
  (a) lawful visitors (Occupiers' Liability Act-equivalent of Foxbridge);
  (b) trespassers, in respect of known dangers (limited duty).

## Part 4 — Mortgages and securities

### §9 Mortgage

(1) A mortgage is a charge on real property securing a debt or other obligation.

(2) Foxbridge mortgages are registered at the Land Registry and rank in priority by date of registration (subject to the priority rules in the Foxbridge Land Registration Order).

(3) On default, the mortgagee may exercise the **power of sale** subject to:
  (a) prior notice;
  (b) duty to obtain reasonable price;
  (c) accounting to the mortgagor for any surplus.

### §10 Charges over personal property

Charges over personal property (including digital assets and POL balances) may be created by deed, registered at the **Foxbridge Companies Registry** for institutional debtors and at the **Foxbridge Personal Charges Registry** for individuals.

## Part 5 — Trusts

### §11 Express, resulting, and constructive trusts

(1) An **express trust** is created by the settlor's manifest intention, identifying property, beneficiaries, and trustees. The "three certainties" doctrine applies.

(2) A **resulting trust** arises by presumption where property is transferred without consideration or where an express trust fails.

(3) A **constructive trust** is imposed by court order where it would be unconscionable for the legal owner to deny the beneficial owner's interest.

### §12 Trustee duties

A trustee owes duties of:
  (a) **loyalty** (no unauthorised conflict, no profit);
  (b) **prudence** (act as a reasonably prudent person);
  (c) **disclosure** to beneficiaries on reasonable request;
  (d) **even-handedness** between beneficiaries with competing interests.

### §13 Pension and image-rights trusts

Footballer image-rights trusts and pension trusts are subject to this Part plus the relevant statutory regime (Foxbridge Pensions Act for pensions; Foxbridge Income Tax (Earnings) Act for image rights).

## Part 6 — Citation, commencement, and extent

### §14 Short title, commencement, and extent

(1) This Act may be cited as the **Foxbridge Property Act**.

(2) This Act came into force on **2026-08-01** in-world.

(3) This Act extends to England-on-Polis only.
